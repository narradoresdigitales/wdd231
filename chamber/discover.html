Economy

La Pampa, traditionally Argentina's leading agricultural province, generated an estimated US$3.144 billion in 2006, with a GDP per capita of US$14,000, which is above the national average.

Agriculture contributes to 25% of the province's economy, with cattle ranching being the most significant activity. Other important agricultural products include wheat, sunflower, and maize in the northeast.

In addition to agriculture, La Pampa has dairy farming, honey production, salt extraction, and numerous oil and gas wells. Despite its potential, tourism remains underdeveloped. Popular attractions include Santa Rosa, Lihué Calel National Park, and various estancias dedicated to agritourism.
