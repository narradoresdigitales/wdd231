My Journey Into Art

Despite not receiving formal art education, my passion for creating and exploring the world through art has been a lifelong journey. Growing up surrounded by nature, I found inspiration in the subtle beauty of landscapes, plants, and the changing seasons. I first picked up pastels as a way to capture the soft light and delicate details of the natural world. Over the years, this medium became my voice, a way to communicate the serenity and wonder I feel in the outdoors. Through practice, dedication, and an unwavering love for nature, I have refined my technique to bring these natural elements to life.

Mentorship Under Mario Venturi

One of the most pivotal moments in my artistic development was meeting Mario Venturi, a renowned artist celebrated for his watercolor work and iconic movie posters. Venturi became my mentor, offering guidance, encouragement, and invaluable insights into the world of art. His experience, particularly his mastery of light and composition, greatly influenced my work, even as I focused on pastels. Venturi never imposed strict rules but instead nurtured my individuality as an artist, pushing me to embrace my own vision while helping me hone my technical skills.

A Commitment to Nature's Beauty

Today, my work remains deeply rooted in nature. I aim to capture not only the visual beauty of landscapes but also the emotions they evoke. Whether it is the delicate rustle of leaves in the wind or the peaceful stillness of a sunset, I strive to translate these fleeting moments into lasting pieces of art. My pastel drawings are more than representations of nature; they are meditations on the connection we all share with the earth. Through my work, I hope to invite viewers into this world of tranquility and reflection, offering a moment of pause in our busy lives.

Connection to The Met

My connection to The Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met) is deeply rooted in its vast collection, which spans centuries and inspires my pastel creations. Influenced by the serene landscapes and vibrant colors found in the museum's artworks, I strive to capture the beauty of nature in my own pieces, drawing parallels with artists like J.M.W. Turner, Claude Monet, and van Gogh.

The Met also serves as a vital hub for artistic dialogue, offering workshops and programs that foster community engagement and collaboration among artists. Ultimately, the museum enriches my understanding of art and fuels my passion for creative expression.
